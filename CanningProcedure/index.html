🫙 Smoked Salsa Canning Procedure

Flavor, safety, and shelf stability — perfected in every jar.

Smoke Blend & Acidify Prepare Jars Fill Jars Pasteurize Seal & Store
Smoke tomatoes, peppers, onions, garlic, and optional fruits at 250–300°F for 1–2 hrs using mesquite or hickory. Blend to desired texture. Add fresh lime juice and food-grade citric acid until pH ≤ 4.5 (target 4.1–4.3). Simmer 10–15 mins.
Test pH with a calibrated meter or quality strips before jarring.		 Wash jars, lids, and rings in hot soapy water; rinse well. Sanitize jars and keep warm until filling. Use new lids. Ladle salsa into jars, leaving ½" headspace. Wipe rims clean. Apply lid and band finger-tight. Water Bath: Fully submerge jars in boiling water (212°F). Process for 15 minutes after water returns to a full boil. Maintain water above jar tops. Cool jars undisturbed for 12–24 hrs. Check seals — lids should be concave and not flex.
Label:
  • “Picante Pequin — Sealed Date”
  • “Picante Pequin — Flavor & Product Info QR”
Store sealed jars in a cool, dry, dark place (55–70°F). Shelf life: 12–18 months unopened.
Smoking deepens flavor and aroma; softens texture for blending. Acidification ensures shelf safety. pH ≤ 4.6 required; ≤ 4.5 preferred for margin. Warm jars prevent thermal shock; clean rims ensure reliable seals. Correct headspace reduces spoilage risk; clean rims improve seal success. Consistent temperature and full submersion are critical for safety. Unsealed jars: refrigerate and consume promptly.
